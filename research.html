Who Benefits from Paid Family Leave? Effects on Unpaid Work

With Kimberly Gan • Under review (2025) • ATUS 2011–2023 • OLS

We estimate how state-level paid family leave policies shape childcare and housework time. Results show policy generosity increases childcare time among eligible groups without crowding out partners’ contributions.

Preprint · Slides · Replication materials

Mapping Belief Structure Change Through Deliberation

Sociological Perspectives (R&R, 2025) • Correlational Class Analysis

We track how belief structures about nuclear energy shift during public deliberation and how those shifts relate to final decisions.

Manuscript · Data

Occupational Composition and Health Inequality

Dissertation project • 3-paper format

Examines how gender and racial/ethnic compositional change in occupations affects inequalities in work conditions, health, and family outcomes over time.
